basically i completely underestimated the card game part - didn't break down all of what it needed. since i understood the rules myself, they were all a given - things like following suit are completely non-obvious and weren't planned for
especially given the fast progress at the start, i set my expectations much higher than they ended up should have been. my todo list was always super short, and i just noticed one thing wrong, fixed that, for 5 hours
didn't get to the cool and difficult part, the roguelike twists
didn't get to the cool and new parts, the charactizeration, the sprites, the dialogue

my instincts for introducing each mechanic slowly, with the devil dialogue boxes, were great - all the feedback i got was confirming that was what i needed to do, and i know that's all i need to do

workflow wise, did an alright job. the lack of planning really bit me, felt pretty burnt how quite quickly - and in non-overwhelming part. when i had flappy bird for example, long to do list, do some until diminishing returns, take a break, get back to it at near 100%. this was just a long set of "alright we're done, just one more thing" forever. didn't project manage super well, since i fell short of even basic expectations before the end - the goal was 1. get basics, 2. add some characterization, 3. add some roguelike mechanics. figured i'd do a first pass of 2 and 3, not a ton. didn't even finish 1 - so the tutorialization was fucked. salvaged it fairly well at the end, with the turn delays and the block of text

pretty funny i'm on game number 4 which has barely ascended past a copy. i suppose i do really like execution over innovation, what with the game reviews and all. i do keep saying "something unique this time", which maybe is just doing my work a disservice.
